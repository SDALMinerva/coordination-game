This is an experiment to study how individuals and groups make decisions. Your earnings in the experiment will depend on your own decisions, the decisions of others, and chance. During the experiment, you will earn experimental points. At the end of the experiment, these points will be converted to cash.

General Instructions:

The experiment consists of 15 rounds in which you and other players independently make the decision on whether to participate in a group event. Examples could include going to a social or a cultural event (e.g., party, concert), playing in team sports (e.g., volleyball, football) or attending a political event (e.g., march, rally). These are events where you are more willing to participate if there are others participating with you. A specific type of event will not be given in this experiment. However, at the end of each round, every player will be asked to make a decision to participate or not participate, and that decision will affect the earnings you receive.

At the beginning of each round, you will be assigned to a new group that consists of 5 players total (including yourself). You will see the list of 5 players in your group on the screen. Your earnings depend on your decision to participate and the participation decision of other players in your group.

Threshold (T):

In each round, each player will be assigned a threshold, T. This threshold, T, represents the number of other players that you need in order to make it worthwhile for you to participate in the group event (see earnings in a round described below). Each player will be assigned either a low threshold of T=1 or high threshold of T=3. For example, if your threshold in a round is T=1, it means that you need at least 1 other player from your group; if your threshold is T=3, you need at least 3 other players from your group to participate in order to make it worthwhile to participate in the group event.

Earnings in a round:

Your earnings in a round depend on your threshold, your participation decision and the decisions of other players in your group.

(a) If your threshold is T=1:

  • If you participate and at least 1 other player participates, then your earnings in the round will be {{session.config.payoff_above_threshold|c}}.
  • If you participate and no one else participates, then your earnings in the round will be {{session.config.payoff_below_threshold|c}}.
  • If you do not participate, your earnings in the round will be {{session.config.payoff_no_participate|c}} regardless of what others do.

(b) If your threshold is T=3:

  • If you participate and at least 3 other players participate, then your earnings in the round will be {{session.config.payoff_above_threshold|c}}.
  • If you participate and less than 3 other players participate, then your earnings in the round will be {{session.config.payoff_below_threshold|c}}.
  • If you do not participate, your earnings in the round will be {{session.config.payoff_no_participate|c}} regardless of what others do.

Each round is independent from the other: whatever happens in a round does not affect the next round. You will not observe the outcome of the rounds during the game until the end of the experiment.

Final (Cash) Earnings:

After completing all the rounds, we will choose ONE random round to determine your final earnings. 1 experimental point is equivalent to 0.15 US dollars. Thus, for your decisions, your total earnings could be: $0, $7.5, or $15.00 depending on the outcome of the randomly chosen round. Because each round is independent from the others and equally likely to be chosen for your payment, you should pay close attention to your decisions in each round.
