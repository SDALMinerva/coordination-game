Please read this consent agreement carefully, and feel free to ask questions to the researchers before you decide to participate in the study.

Purpose of Research: To help better understand individual and group decision-making; to learn about communication mechanisms, networks, and decisions that give rise to collective action.

The researchers are faculty members and students at Virginia Tech (VT) and Claremont Graduate University (CGU) and we anticipate recruiting approximately 2000 subjects from these and surrounding universities.

Criteria for Participation: You must be at least 18 years of age to participate in this study.

Procedures: The experiment has three parts: an initial survey, the experiment, and a post-survey about preferences and choices. Both will be conducted using a computer. In the initial survey part, you will be asked to provide demographic and other information. In the experiment part, we will evaluate decisions over some simple games. Prior to the game, you will be presented with detailed instructions (a series of screens on the web) and examples that explain the nature of the experimental task and how decisions affect compensation. This will be followed by a quiz to check understanding of the instructions and the compensation structure. You will then make a number of decisions which will determine your compensation. The compensation depends on both your decisions and the decisions of the other participants in the experiment. At the end of this decision-making stage, you will participate in a set of experimental tasks and questions that will probe your perceptions of the experiment.

Time required: You will spend about 1-2 hours in the session today.  There is no obligation to return for any additional sessions.  However, you may be asked to volunteer again in the future to participate in other experiments.

Risks: There are no anticipated risks.

Benefits: There will be no direct benefits to you for participating in this study.  However, your participation will help us learn about decision-making.

Compensation: The minimum amount you will earn is $7 (including the show-up fee of $7).  Total earnings will differ from person to person, they will range between $7 and $25 (including the show-up fee).

Right to withdraw from the study: You have the right to withdraw from the study at any time without penalty.  

How to withdraw from the study: If you want to withdraw from the study, tell the experimenter quietly and leave the room.  There is no penalty for withdrawing and you will still earn the $7.00 show-up fee.  

Voluntary Participation: Your participation in the study is completely voluntary. You may stop or withdraw from the study at any time without it being held against you. You are free to decline to answer any particular question you do not wish to answer for any reason. Your decision whether or not to participate will have no effect on your current or future connection with anyone at any university. Refusal to participate will involve no penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled (i.e. your standing in school or in any of your classes will not be affected by your participation in the study).

Anonymity and Confidentiality: Your individual privacy will be protected in all papers, books, talks, posts, or stories resulting from this study. In order to protect the confidentiality of your responses, we will assign you an identification (ID) number, and your decisions and the collected data will be recorded using that number without a link to your name and other identifiable information. The ID number will be stored in a separate database which can only be accessed by specifically authorized personnel.

Your name will not be used in any report and we will obtain no information linking your name to the ID number. The results of the study, including laboratory or any other data, may be published for scientific purposes and may be shared with other researchers, but we will not give your name or any identifiable references of you.

At no time will the researchers release identifiable results of the study to anyone other than individuals working on the project without your written consent. The only exceptions are DoD and Federal government personnel and the VT Institutional Review Board (IRB) who oversee the protection of human subjects and may request to audit the study data.

Questions or concerns: If you have any questions or would like additional information about this study, please contact Gizem Korkmaz, Biocomplexity Institute of Virginia Tech, phone: (571) 858-3113, e-mail: gkorkmaz@bi.vt.edu; or Monica Capra, Claremont Graduate University, phone: (909) 607-3372, e-mail: monica.capra@cgu.edu.
